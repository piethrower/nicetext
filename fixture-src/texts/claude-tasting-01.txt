RED WINES

Domaine Lefevre, Volnay 1er Cru "Les Caillerets," 2018. A pale ruby in the glass, edging toward garnet at the rim. The nose opens with crushed raspberry and dried rose petal, then deepens into a savoury core of forest floor and faint white pepper. The palate is silken, almost diaphanous, with fine-grained tannins that resolve into a long, mineral finish. A wine of restraint rather than display; will reward another six to eight years in the cellar.

Chateau Veyrac, Saint-Estephe, 2015. Deep, brooding crimson, opaque at the centre. Cedar, graphite, and dark cassis on the nose; a faint note of the leather chair in a country library. The palate is structured but unhurried, with grippy tannins that loosen across an hour in the decanter. The finish is long, savoury, and threaded with iron. Drinks well now but will be a different wine, possibly a better one, in 2030.

Tenuta della Quercia Vecchia, Brunello di Montalcino, 2016. A garnet of unusual depth, with the orange-tinged rim that distinguishes the better Sangiovese. Sour cherry, dried tobacco, and balsamic herbs dominate the nose, with a discreet undertone of cured meat. The palate is muscular but proportional; tannins are present but well-mannered, framed by bright acidity that keeps the whole structure from settling. The finish carries a faint and not unwelcome bitterness of orange peel.

Bodega San Telmo, Mendoza Malbec, 2021. Inky purple in the glass, almost black at the centre. The nose is plush with blueberry, violet, and a frank sweetness of vanilla from the new oak. The palate follows accordingly: full, ripe, and densely fruited, with soft tannins and a finish that fades quickly into warmth. A wine for immediate pleasure rather than reflection; pairs well with grilled beef and uncomplicated company.

Weingut Hofmann, Spatburgunder, 2019. A delicate pale ruby, almost translucent. Strawberry leaf, blood orange, and a faintly herbal lift on the nose, suggesting dried tarragon. The palate is light-bodied, exact, and almost weightless; tannins are barely perceptible; the acidity is the wine's principal architecture. Finishes with a saline twist that surprises on first encounter and pleases on second.

Quinta da Pedra Solta, Douro Reserva, 2017. A dense, almost black ruby, with viscous tears that descend slowly down the glass. The nose offers crushed blackberry, eucalyptus, and a sweetly tarry note of woodsmoke, characteristic of the Douro's schistose soils. The palate is rich, layered, and built upon firm tannins that promise longevity; the acidity is generous but well-integrated. The finish is long and faintly bitter, in the agreeable manner of dark chocolate.

Domaine Boucher, Chinon "Le Clos des Renards," 2020. A vivid, light-catching purple. Crushed blueberry, pencil shavings, and the unmistakable green note that distinguishes Cabernet Franc on the limestone of the Loire; some find it herbaceous, others speak of bell pepper. The palate is bright, lifted, and unburdened by either oak or pretension. A wine to be drunk young, slightly chilled, and without ceremony.

Cantina Antoniucci, Aglianico del Vulture, 2014. A deep garnet shading to brick. The nose is savoury and meditative: dried fig, leather, dark cherry, and a curious note of fennel pollen. The palate is broad, structured, and somewhat austere, with tannins that resolve slowly across the meal; this is not a wine that flatters from the first sip. The finish is long, dry, and faintly smoky. Best decanted; better still, opened the night before.

Bodegas Aldama, Rioja Gran Reserva, 2010. A mature garnet, with a brick-orange rim that signals its years. The nose is unmistakably traditional: vanilla, coconut, sweet tobacco, and stewed strawberry, the unmistakable signature of long aging in American oak. The palate is silken, integrated, and at peace with itself. The finish is long, gentle, and quietly dignified. A wine that has finished arguing.

Penfolds Bin 389, South Australia, 2018. A dense, polished crimson. Blackcurrant, dark chocolate, and the faintly menthol lift that distinguishes the better Australian blends; the oak is present but no longer dominant. The palate is generous, ripe, and structured by firm but unaggressive tannins; the acidity is sufficient if not striking. A wine of confident ambition, made for the long table and the patient meal.

Domaine Vaissier-Mougeot, Gigondas, 2019. A dark cherry red. Garrigue, black olive, and crushed kirsch on the nose; the palate is muscular, warm, and frank, with tannins that ride along on a tide of ripe fruit. The finish is long, savoury, and faintly peppery. Drinks like a wine that has been working all day in the sun, and has earned its glass.

Heitz-Lochardet, Pommard "Les Petits Noizons," 2020. Light, transparent ruby. The nose is layered and somewhat reticent on first opening: red plum, cocoa nib, dried lavender, and an animal note that the Burgundian masters call sous-bois. The palate is poised, lifted, and structured by acidity rather than tannin; the finish is long and quietly insistent. A wine that improves over the course of the bottle, and again the next afternoon.

Bichi, "Pet Mex" Carignan, Baja California, 2021. Bright, almost violet purple. Crushed cherry, dried hibiscus, blood orange, and a frank earthy note that places the wine immediately. The palate is bright, fresh, and made without intervention; some bottle variation is reported and indeed welcomed by the producer. The finish is short, lifted, and amusing in the best sense.

Domaine de la Pergola, Cote-Rotie "La Chatillonne," 2017. A dark, garnet-tinged ruby. Smoked bacon, violet, white pepper, and the unmistakable Syrah signature of crushed black olive. The palate is exact, layered, and built around an acid line that never falters; the tannins are firm but velvety, and the oak, though present, is wholly digested. The finish is long, layered, and faintly floral.

Tenuta Sant'Anna, Chianti Classico Riserva, 2018. A bright ruby of medium intensity. Sour cherry, bay leaf, and a cured-meat savoury note dominate the nose; the palate is taut, dry, and unsweetened, with tannins that grip without bullying. The finish is long, savoury, and finishes on a bitter-orange note that lingers agreeably.

Domaine du Vieux Lavoir, Cornas, 2016. Inky, opaque purple. Black olive, smoked meat, dark cassis, and the faintly tarry note of granitic Syrah. The palate is dense, brooding, and structured by tannins that will not yield to anything less than a roast and seven more years in the cellar. The finish is long, savoury, and faintly austere; the wine does not flatter, but it does not need to.

Bodega Errazuriz, Aconcagua Cabernet, 2017. A polished garnet with a faintly violet edge. Cassis, eucalyptus, graphite, and a sweet undertone of vanilla on the nose; the oak is integrated though still apparent. The palate is broad, ripe, and confidently structured; tannins are firm and well-managed. The finish is long, faintly minty, and inclined to outlast the meal.

Domaine Pierre-Yves Colin-Morey, Saint-Aubin "En Remilly," 2020 (red). A pale and almost surprising ruby; Saint-Aubin in red is rare, and worth the curiosity. The nose is fine-grained: red currant, white pepper, a thread of jasmine. The palate is lifted, almost weightless, and ends on a saline note that flatters the food rather than competing with it.

Cantina Terlano, Lagrein Riserva, 2018. A deep, almost black ruby. The nose is savoury and slightly wild: black cherry, juniper, and the faintly metallic note that distinguishes Lagrein at its best. The palate is structured and slightly rustic, with tannins that demand a meal. The finish is long, savoury, and reminds the drinker that the Alps are not far off.

Mas Doix, Priorat "Salanques," 2017. Inky, dense, almost black. Crushed blackberry, dried fig, dark chocolate, and the unmistakable mineral note of llicorella schist. The palate is muscular, layered, and structured by both tannin and acidity; the alcohol is present but well-clothed. A wine of seriousness, not subtlety; rewards both decanting and patience.

Domaine Tempier, Bandol Rouge, 2019. A deep, brooding garnet. Garrigue, black cherry, leather, and a faintly meaty note that places the wine in Provence and nowhere else. The palate is structured, savoury, and built for the long haul; the tannins are firm but never aggressive. The finish is long, lifted, and finishes on a note of dried herbs.
