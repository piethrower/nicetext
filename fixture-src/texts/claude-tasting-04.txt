SPARKLING WINES AND CHAMPAGNES

Champagne Egly-Ouriet, Grand Cru Ambonnay, "Les Crayeres," disgorged March 2022. A pale gold of unusual depth, with a fine and exceptionally persistent mousse that climbs in steady columns rather than rushed plumes. The nose is layered: brioche, lemon curd, a thread of toasted almond, and the white-flower lift that distinguishes the better blanc de noirs from Ambonnay. The palate is taut, vinous, and chalky, with the dosage so discreet as to be scarcely perceptible; the finish is long, saline, and threaded with iodine. A wine of restraint rather than display; will reward another four years on the cork after disgorgement.

Pierre Peters, Le Mesnil-sur-Oger, "Cuvee de Reserve" Blanc de Blancs, NV. Bright lemon in the glass, with a perlage that climbs steadily and refuses to subside. The nose offers crushed green apple, fresh lime peel, white pistachio, and the faintly oyster-shell minerality that places it instantly on the chalk of the Cotes des Blancs. The palate is precise, bone-dry, and unburdened by either oak or pretension; the mousse is fine and persistent. The finish is chalky and exact, with a cool length that flatters seafood and discourages conversation.

Krug, "Grande Cuvee" 170eme Edition, disgorged 2020. A deeper amber than expected, with a slow, fine mousse that suggests the long lees aging the house insists upon. Roasted hazelnut, candied citrus peel, dried chamomile, and the unmistakable Krug signature of buttered toast and faintly acetic preserved lemon. The palate is broad, vinous, and structured around an oxidative axis that will divide the room; the autolytic notes are pronounced, and some will find them excessive. The finish is long, savoury, and quietly authoritative.

Larmandier-Bernier, Vertus 1er Cru "Terre de Vertus" Non Dose, 2018. A pale straw with a green tinge; the bead is fine and persistent, climbing in patient columns. The nose is herbal and saline: fennel frond, white grapefruit pith, crushed oyster shell, and a thread of beeswax. The palate is bone-dry, exact, and faintly austere, with no dosage to soften its edges; the wine demands food and rewards it. The finish is chalky, mineral, and long. Best served at ten degrees, in a tulip rather than a flute.

Champagne Selosse, "Substance," disgorged 2019. A burnished gold, almost amber at the rim, with a discreet bead that some will read as fatigue and others as elegance. The nose is profound and divisive: oxidative apple, walnut skin, cured lemon, and a frank sherried lift that the house has long since stopped apologising for. The palate is dense, vinous, and structurally closer to a great white burgundy than to the conventional Champagne template; the mousse is barely there. A wine that the producer's admirers will defend at length and his detractors dismiss at greater length still.

Bollinger, "La Grande Annee," 2014. A pale gold with a steady, fine mousse. Brioche, yellow apple, candied ginger, and a confidently oaky lift; the autolytic character is generous without tipping into excess. The palate is broad, structured, and built upon the firm Pinot Noir spine that distinguishes the house. The finish is long, savoury, and threaded with toasted almond. Drinks well now but will be a different wine, possibly a finer one, in 2030.

Champagne Jacquesson, "Cuvee 745" Extra Brut. A pale lemon-gold with a steady, persistent bead. The nose offers green apple, crushed pear, chamomile, and a thread of toasted brioche. The palate is precise and vinous, with the firm acidity that the house's house style demands; the dosage, at three grams per litre, scarcely registers. The finish is long, dry, and faintly savoury. A wine of architecture rather than ornament.

Roederer, "Cristal," 2014. A pale gold with a notably fine mousse; the bead is exact, persistent, and visually almost ascetic. The nose is restrained on first opening: white flower, crushed almond, lemon zest, a faintly chalky lift. With air the wine deepens into pastry, dried apricot, and a discreet honeyed undertone. The palate is taut, structured, and unmistakably patrician; the finish is long, mineral, and unhurried. A wine that does not announce itself, and is the better for it.

Champagne Salon, Le Mesnil-sur-Oger, 2008. Pale gold with a green-tinged rim; the mousse is fine and unhurriedly persistent. The nose is profound: white truffle, crushed hazelnut, beeswax, candied lemon peel, and the saline, almost iodine-tinged minerality that the chalk of Le Mesnil reliably produces in great years. The palate is dense, structured, and vinous to a degree that makes the bubbles seem incidental. The finish is exceptionally long, chalky, and faintly austere. A wine to be drunk slowly and at temperature, with very little else on the table.

Diebolt-Vallois, Cramant, "Fleur de Passion," 2015. A pale gold with a fine, lively perlage. White peach, almond paste, jasmine, and crushed chalk on the nose; the palate is creamy, layered, and structured around a precise acid line that the village reliably delivers. The dosage is discreet; the finish is long, mineral, and threaded with white flower. A wine of poise.

Champagne Cedric Bouchard, "Roses de Jeanne" Les Ursules, 2018. A pale salmon, the colour deepening at the centre. Crushed strawberry leaf, blood orange, white pepper, and a faintly herbal lift. The palate is light, lifted, and almost weightless, with an acid line so exact that the wine seems to hover. The finish is long, saline, and disconcertingly fine. A wine of singular voice.

Marie-Courtin, Cote des Bar, "Resonance" Extra Brut, 2018. Pale lemon, with a fine, steady bead. The nose is reductive on first opening: struck flint, white grapefruit pith, fennel frond. With air it opens into apple skin, crushed almond, and a thread of beeswax. The palate is bone-dry, vinous, and structured by the Aube's particular Pinot Noir signature; the dosage is non-existent. The finish is long, chalky, and faintly austere.

The palate is broad, generous, and structured by a firm Pinot spine; the dosage is well-judged. The finish is long, gently honeyed, and quietly pleased with itself.

A wine for the long evening and the patient interlocutor.

The palate is broad, structured, and confident; the bead is fine. The finish is long, savoury, and threaded with the faintly bitter note of orange peel.

Champagne Bereche et Fils, Ludes Premier Cru, "Les Beaux Regards," 2017. A pale gold with a fine, persistent perlage. The nose is precise: yellow apple, crushed almond, white flower, and a faintly chalky lift. The palate is taut, vinous, and lifted; the dosage is discreet, the bead is fine. The finish is long, mineral, and quietly authoritative. A wine of architecture.

Champagne Suenen, Cramant Grand Cru, "Oiry" Extra Brut, 2018. A pale gold with a fine, steady mousse. The nose is composed: white flower, crushed almond, fennel pollen, and a discreet pastry-crust lift. The palate is bone-dry, vinous, and structured around the chalk of the Cotes des Blancs; the dosage is non-existent. The finish is long, chalky, and threaded with iodine.

Champagne Gosset, "Celebris" Extra Brut, 2007. A pale gold with a fine, exceptionally persistent mousse. The nose is layered and patient: brioche, candied lemon peel, dried apricot, crushed hazelnut, and a thread of beeswax. The palate is dense, vinous, and structured around an acid line that does not falter; the dosage is discreet. The finish is exceptionally long, chalky, and quietly authoritative. A wine that has finished arguing.
