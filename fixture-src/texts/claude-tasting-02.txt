WHITE WINES

Weingut Muller-Scharzhof, Saar Riesling Kabinett, 2021. A pale lemon with green flashes, almost watery at the rim. The nose is precise and slightly cold: green apple, wet slate, white peach, and a faintly smoky note that the Saar gives without asking. The palate is taut and lifted, with a thread of residual sugar that the acidity dispatches before the next sip. The finish is long, mineral, and threaded with chalk; a wine of restraint rather than display.

Domaine Trimbach, Riesling "Cuvee Frederic Emile," Alsace, 2017. Bright straw, with a faint glint of gold beginning to assert itself. The nose offers crushed lime, honeysuckle, and the unmistakable petrol note that Alsace gives at five years and never apologises for. The palate is dry, dense, and structured by an acid line that runs the length of the wine; the texture is waxy without being heavy. Better with another four years on the cork; impatient drinkers will find it too austere.

Grosset, "Polish Hill" Riesling, Clare Valley, 2022. A pale lemon-gold, brilliant in the glass. Crushed lime leaf, white grapefruit, and a curiously dry pebbly note dominate the nose; there is no oak, no malolactic, no hesitation. The palate is racy, almost severe, with the kind of acidity that scrubs the palate clean of whatever preceded it. The finish is long and saline. A wine that has finished arguing.

Domaine Raveneau, Chablis 1er Cru "Montee de Tonnerre," 2019. A pale lemon, cool-toned, with a discreet sheen. The nose is reticent on first opening: oyster shell, lemon pith, and a faint note of struck flint that is not affectation but Kimmeridgian fact. The palate is taut, mineral, and almost severe; the fruit is present but secondary to the structure. The finish is long, chalky, and persistent. Drinks well now but will be a different wine in 2032.

Domaine Coche-Dury, Meursault "Les Rougeots," 2018. A deep straw with a faint green edge. The nose is layered and slow to unfold: hazelnut, lemon curd, brioche, and the faintly smoky reduction that the house has made into a signature. The palate is broad, dense, and structured by an acid line that holds the considerable weight of the fruit aloft. The oak is present though no longer dominant. A wine of confident ambition; the price is, of course, another matter.

Domaine Leflaive, Puligny-Montrachet "Les Pucelles," 2017. A pale lemon-gold of considerable brilliance. The nose offers white flowers, crushed almond, white peach, and a thread of acacia honey. The palate is precise, lifted, and built around an exact seam of acidity; the texture is silken without being plush. The finish is long, mineral, and threaded with iodine. A wine of restraint that will reward another five years in the cellar; opened too young, it shows only its bones.

Domaine Pierre-Yves Colin-Morey, Saint-Aubin 1er Cru "En Remilly," 2020. Bright straw with green flashes. The nose is fine-grained: green apple, lemon zest, white blossom, and a faint chalk dust note that places the wine on its hill. The palate is taut and lifted, with the kind of phenolic grip that frames the fruit rather than competing with it. The finish is long and saline. Saint-Aubin remains the patient drinker's Puligny, and this bottle does the appellation no harm.

Domaine des Comtes Lafon, Macon-Milly-Lamartine, 2021. A pale lemon, almost watery in the glass. Yellow apple, white pear, and a thread of crushed hazelnut on the nose; no oak makes itself felt, though a hint of lees-derived breadiness lingers. The palate is lifted, exact, and almost weightless, with acidity that runs cleanly to a saline finish. A wine of unforced charm; pairs well with cold chicken and warm conversation.

Domaine Didier Dagueneau, Pouilly-Fume "Silex," 2019. A bright straw of unusual depth. The nose is slow to unfold and rewards patience: smoke, white peach, lime peel, and a discreet note of toasted almond from the old oak. The palate is broad, layered, and structured by acidity rather than weight; this is Sauvignon Blanc as a serious wine, not a fashion. The finish is long, mineral, and threaded with white pepper. Better with another three years on the cork.

Domaine de la Pepiere, Muscadet Sevre-et-Maine "Clos des Briords," 2020. A pale lemon with a faint silver edge. Crushed oyster shell, green apple, lemon pith, and a saline note that the Atlantic seems to have written into the wine directly. The palate is taut and almost weightless, with a phenolic grip from the lees that gives the wine more body than the colour suggests. The finish is long, salty, and faintly bitter. Pairs with shellfish and very little else; this is not a defect.

Domaine Huet, Vouvray "Le Mont" Sec, 2019. A bright straw, brilliant in the glass. The nose is layered: quince, beeswax, chamomile, and a thread of wet stone. The palate is dense, waxy, and structured by an acid line that the Chenin holds with characteristic patience; the fruit is ripe but never ripe enough to slacken. The finish is long, mineral, and faintly bitter. A wine that improves over the course of the bottle; the second day is sometimes the better one.

Domaine Huet, Vouvray "Clos du Bourg" Demi-Sec, 2018. A pale gold beginning to deepen. The nose offers ripe quince, honeycomb, dried apricot, and a faintly mushroomy note of botrytis kept in its place. The palate is generous, layered, and structured by acidity that disposes of the residual sugar before it can settle. The finish is long, honeyed, and threaded with chalk. Demi-sec done as it should be done; the residual sugar is present but well-managed.

Domaine du Closel, Savennieres "Clos du Papillon," 2017. A deep straw with golden glints. The nose is meditative and slow to give: bruised apple, beeswax, dried chamomile, and the faintly oxidative note that Savennieres at its best does not avoid. The palate is dense, waxy, and almost stern; the fruit is secondary to the structure. The finish is long, mineral, and faintly bitter. A wine of seriousness, not subtlety; rewards food and patience in roughly equal measure.

Pieropan, Soave Classico "Calvarino," 2021. A pale lemon with a faint green tint. The nose is fine-grained and unhurried: white pear, almond blossom, crushed marjoram, and a thread of volcanic stone. The palate is lifted, exact, and built around an acid line that holds the fruit in proportion; the texture is faintly waxy. The finish is long, almondy, and finishes on a faintly bitter note that the Garganega gives without apology.

Bucci, Verdicchio dei Castelli di Jesi Classico Superiore, 2020. A pale lemon with green flashes. The nose offers green apple, fennel pollen, lemon pith, and a faintly herbal lift that the Verdicchio gives at its best. The palate is taut and structured, with a phenolic grip that frames the fruit; the texture is lean rather than waxy. The finish is long, almondy, and finishes on a discreet bitter note. Better with another two years on the cork.

Villa Bucci Riserva, Verdicchio, 2018. A deeper straw, beginning to gold. The nose is layered and slow to unfold: ripe pear, beeswax, dried herbs, and a thread of toasted almond. The palate is broad, waxy, and structured by an acid line that holds the considerable weight of the wine aloft. The finish is long, mineral, and finishes on a saline note. Verdicchio as a serious wine; the bottle does what the appellation has been threatening to do for decades.

Borgo del Tiglio, Friulano "Ronco della Chiesa," 2019. A pale gold, brilliant in the glass. The nose is precise and faintly Continental: white peach, almond blossom, crushed marjoram, and a thread of paraffin. The palate is dense, waxy, and structured by an acid line that the Friulano keeps without effort; the texture is silken rather than plush. The finish is long, almondy, and faintly bitter. A wine of unforced confidence.

Mastroberardino, Greco di Tufo, 2021. A pale lemon-gold. The nose offers ripe pear, white blossom, crushed hazelnut, and a discreet sulphur note that the volcanic soils give and that disappears with a swirl. The palate is broad, lifted, and structured by acidity rather than weight; the texture is faintly waxy. The finish is long, mineral, and threaded with smoke. Pairs well with grilled fish and the kind of conversation that does not require an opinion.

Pietracupa, Fiano di Avellino, 2020. A pale straw with a faint green edge. The nose is layered: ripe pear, beeswax, crushed almond, and a thread of wild fennel. The palate is dense, waxy, and built around an acid line that runs cleanly through the wine; the fruit is ripe but disciplined. The finish is long, savoury, and faintly bitter. A wine that improves with another three years on the cork; the impatient drinker is encouraged to decant.

Cantina Lunae, Vermentino "Etichetta Nera," Colli di Luni, 2022. A pale lemon with a faint silver edge. The palate is lifted, exact, and almost weightless, with a phenolic grip on the finish that gives the wine more substance than the colour suggests. The finish is long and salty.
