A useful finishing salt for grilled meat and for soups that need the depth without the actual smoking; the producer has resisted the temptation to over-smoke, and is to be commended.

Pink Himalayan Salt, supermarket bottle. A pale pink in coarse crystals. The nose is, broadly speaking, of nothing. The palate is bracing, saline, and finishes cleanly. The trace minerals of which the marketing makes much are, on chemical analysis, negligible; the colour is the principal selling point. A salt; one wishes one paid for the salt alone.

Comte AOP, Fort Saint-Antoine, 36-month, Marcel Petite. A pale straw-yellow paste of dense, slightly granular texture. The nose is layered: hazelnut, brown butter, dried apricot, a faintly resinous mountain lift. The palate is balanced, structured, and finishes long; the crystalline crunch of the calcium lactate is well-distributed. A Comte of seriousness; the cellar at Fort Saint-Antoine maintains conditions that the lowland producers do not.

Parmigiano Reggiano DOP, "Vacche Rosse," 36-month, Caseificio Rosola. A pale straw paste of dense, granular texture. The nose is unmistakable: brown butter, pineapple, dried fruit, a discreet meaty lift. The palate is balanced, structured, and finishes long; the crystalline crunch is the principal textural architecture. A Parmigiano of considerable seriousness; the vacche rosse herd is small, and the cheese is correspondingly so.

Beemster, Aged Gouda XO, 26-month, Netherlands. A deep amber paste of dense crystalline texture. The nose is layered: caramel, butterscotch, a faint meaty lift, a discreet sweetness. The palate is balanced, sweet-savoury, and finishes long. A cheese of considerable charm; the Dutch have long understood that age in gouda produces sweetness as much as savouriness, and the rest of the world is gradually catching up.

Ossau-Iraty AOP, Petit Agour, Pays Basque, 8-month. A pale yellow paste of dense, slightly oily texture. The nose is layered: lanolin, hazelnut, dried fruit, a discreet floral lift. The palate is balanced, rounded, and finishes long; the saltiness is well-distributed. A sheep's-milk cheese of seriousness; pairs well with black cherry confiture and a sceptical eye.

Stichelton, Nottinghamshire, 16-week. A pale ivory paste with deep blue veining. The nose is layered: cream, mushroom, leather, a discreet barnyard lift. The palate is balanced, savoury, and finishes long; the blue is firm without being aggressive. The unpasteurised sibling of stilton, and worth knowing about for that reason; the producer has done admirable work in preserving the original method.

Epoisses AOP, Berthaut, Burgundy, 8-week. A bright orange-red rind enclosing a pale, almost runny ivory paste. The nose is unrepentant: barnyard, marc, ammonia, a discreet sweetness. The palate is balanced, savoury, and finishes long; the rind is structural. A cheese for those who have committed; pairs well with the marc with which it was washed, and very little else.

Montgomery's Cheddar, Somerset, 18-month. A pale yellow paste of dense, slightly crumbly texture. The nose is layered: brown butter, hay, a faint meaty lift, a discreet acidity. The palate is balanced, savoury, and finishes long. A cheddar of restraint rather than display; pairs well with a properly bitter beer and a sceptical eye.

Vichy Catalan, Caldes de Malavella, Spain. A clear, faintly green-tinged water of pronounced effervescence. The nose, if one may speak of the nose of a water, offers a faintly sulphurous lift and a discreet mineral signature. The palate is bracing, mineral, and finishes long; the salinity is structural, the effervescence integrated. A water for the table of someone who has finished pretending the present register cannot be applied to water.

Apollinaris, Bad Neuenahr, Germany. A clear water of medium effervescence. The nose is faintly mineral, with a discreet salinity. The palate is balanced, mineral, and finishes cleanly; the bubble is fine and persistent. A reliable working sparkling water at a sensible price; the standard against which the more eccentric mineral waters measure themselves.

Acqua Panna, Tuscany. A clear, still water. The nose is essentially neutral. The palate is balanced, mild, and finishes cleanly; the mineral content is low and the texture is silken. A reliable working still water at a sensible price; the standard against which other Italian still waters measure themselves.

Antipodes, Otakiri Springs, New Zealand. A clear, still water of fine clarity. The nose is essentially neutral. The palate is balanced, mild, and finishes cleanly. A water of considerable purity, sold at a price that reflects the carbon footprint rather more than the liquid; the bottle is heavy, the contents are pleasant, and the entire enterprise is faintly absurd. The note nonetheless writes itself.

Cohiba Behike 56, Cuba. A medium brown wrapper of fine texture and considerable oil. The cold draw offers cedar, leather, a faint cocoa lift. On lighting, the smoke is layered: cedar, leather, dried fruit, a discreet sweetness, a faintly resinous lift. The construction is exact, the burn even, the ash a pale grey of considerable length. A cigar of restraint rather than display; the price reflects the legend rather more than the liquid, but the liquid is good.

Partagas Serie D No. 4, Cuba. A medium-dark brown wrapper of fine texture. The cold draw offers earth, cedar, a faint sweetness. On lighting, the smoke is dense and savoury: earth, leather, a faintly peppery lift, a discreet cocoa undertone. The burn is even, the construction exact. A robusto of seriousness; pairs well with a long afternoon and an espresso.

Arturo Fuente, Opus X "Reserva d'Chateau," Dominican Republic. A deep brown wrapper of considerable oil. The cold draw offers cedar, dried fruit, a faint spice lift. On lighting, the smoke is balanced and complex: cedar, dried fruit, a faintly peppery lift, a discreet sweetness. The construction is exact. A cigar of considerable charm; the producer has done admirable work in raising the visibility of the Dominican wrapper, and the bands are designed to be noticed.

Padron 1964 Anniversary "Exclusivo," Nicaragua, maduro. A deep, almost black brown wrapper of considerable oil. The cold draw offers cocoa, coffee, a faint sweetness. On lighting, the smoke is dense and layered: cocoa, espresso, leather, a discreet pepper. The burn is even, the construction exact. A cigar of seriousness; the maduro processing has produced a depth that the natural wrapper cannot match.

Montecristo No. 2, Cuba, aged 7 years. A medium-dark brown wrapper of fine texture. The cold draw offers cedar, leather, a faint cocoa lift. On lighting, the smoke is balanced, complex, and layered; the age has rounded the edges and integrated the components. The construction is exact. A torpedo of considerable seriousness; pairs well with a glass of an old armagnac and a sceptical eye.
