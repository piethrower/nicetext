ROSES AND ORANGE WINES

Domaine de la Mistralerie, Cotes de Provence "Galets Blancs," 2023. A pale onion-skin pink, almost translucent, with the faintest peach undertone that flatters a chilled glass. The nose offers crushed strawberry, white peach, and a discreet note of crushed thyme that places the wine somewhere between Salon-de-Provence and the sea. The palate is taut, dry, and built around an acid line that does the necessary work without theatrics. The finish is short, clean, and saline; pairs well with grilled sardines and unhurried company.

Domaine Tempier, Bandol Rose, 2023. A pale salmon, almost coral, with the seriousness of intent that distinguishes Bandol from its lesser Provencal cousins. The nose is layered: white peach, blood orange, crushed rose petal, and a savoury note of dried herbs and warm stone. The palate is structured, exact, and unusually long for a rose; the Mourvedre asserts itself quietly through a fine grain of phenolic texture. The finish is long, dry, and threaded with a faintly bitter note of grapefruit pith. A wine that knows what it is.

Domaine Pradeaux, Bandol Rose, 2022. A deeper salmon-coral than is currently fashionable, which is to its credit. Crushed strawberry, dried orange peel, garrigue, and a faintly meaty note that the better Bandol roses share with their red siblings. The palate is dense for a rose, broad-shouldered, and structured by a quiet phenolic grip; this is a wine for the table rather than the aperitif hour. The finish is long, savoury, and inclined to lengthen across a meal of grilled lamb.

Chateau Simone, "Palette" Rose, 2022. A pale apricot-pink with the structural seriousness one expects from this small and idiosyncratic appellation. The nose is unusual and rewarding: dried fig, white pepper, blood orange, and a faintly resinous note that suggests warm pine. The palate is broad, layered, and built upon a quiet phenolic frame; this is a rose with the architecture of a white Burgundy. The finish is long, savoury, and faintly bitter in the best sense. A wine of seriousness, not seasonality.

Chateau Miraval, Cotes de Provence, 2023. A pale silvery pink. Crushed strawberry, peach, and a discreet floral note on the nose; the palate is bright, fresh, and entirely free of pretension. Better than the celebrity ownership has any right to suggest, though one is always conscious of paying a small premium for the label.

Domaine Ott, "Clos Mireille" Rose, 2022. A pale apricot-coral in the celebrated tall bottle. The nose is layered and serious: white peach, blood orange, crushed almond, and a thread of rosemary. The palate is broad, structured, and unusually long; the acidity is firm and the texture faintly waxy. The finish is long, dry, and finishes on a note of warm stone. A wine of confident ambition; the price is high but the bottle does not disappoint.

Domaine de la Mordoree, Tavel "La Dame Rousse," 2022. A bright cherry pink, deeper than fashion currently allows, and entirely correct for the appellation. The nose is generous: crushed raspberry, blood orange, dried herbs, and a faint note of white pepper that signals the Grenache. The palate is broad, structured, and faintly phenolic; this is a rose for the table, not the aperitif. The finish is long, dry, and savoury.

Domaine Maby, Tavel "La Forcadiere," 2022. A deep cherry pink with serious intent. Strawberry, watermelon rind, dried orange peel, and a savoury note of crushed thyme on the nose. The palate is full, dry, and structured by a quiet phenolic grip; the alcohol is present but well-clothed. The finish is long, dry, and faintly bitter.

A wine that has finished arguing.
